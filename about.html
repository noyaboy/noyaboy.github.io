About Me

A snapshot of my education, experience, and achievements

Hao-Jyun (Noah) Liang

I am currently pursuing a Master of Engineering in the Integrated Circuits & Systems and Electronic Design Automation Group of the Institute of Pioneer Semiconductor Innovation at National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University, with a focus on Digital VLSI and System Design. My research is carried out in the Parallel Computing System Laboratory under the guidance of Professor Bo-Cheng Lai.

In addition to my academic work, I serve as an Affiliate Trainee on the Accelerated AI Algorithms for Data-Driven Discovery (A3D3) project in Washington, U.S. In this role, I specialize in optimizing GPU algorithms to enhance performance and efficiency in particle trajectory reconstruction for HL-LHC experiments in High-Energy Physics. By addressing complex computational challenges and leveraging advanced GPU platforms, I contribute to real-time data processing and drive breakthroughs in particle physics research.

My undergraduate journey began in the Physics Department at National Tsing Hua University , where I achieved the highest ranking during my first year. I later transferred to the Electrical Engineering Department at National Tsing Hua University and graduated with distinction. Driven by a passion for artificial intelligence and hardware acceleration, I joined Andes Technology as a Digital Design Intern, where I worked on CPU-related AI and deep learning accelerator projects.
